# What Are Common Design Terms to Help You Communicate with Designers?

## Layout
Layout is how the visual elements are arraanged on a page or screen to communicate a message. This includes things like images, text, and white space. The layout is like the blueprint of the design.

## Alignment
After layout is alignment. Alignment is how the elements are placed in relation to one another. Using alignment correctly is helpful for making the design look clean and organized. Designers create visual order by aligning elements along imaginary lines, edges, or a central point.

## Composition & Balance
Composition is the art of arranging elements to create a harmonious design. It determines how elements like images, text, and shapes relate to each other and contribute to the design in an artistic way. 

Composition is closely related to the concept of balance. Balance is how the visual weight is distributed within a composition. Designers aim to create an equilibrium through symmetrical or asymmetrical arrangements. A balanced design feels harmonious.

## Hierarchy
Hierarchy is another important concept. Hierarchy establishes the oorder of importance of the elements in a design. It's about making sure the most important information is noticed first. You can implement a visual hierarchy with size, color, contrast, alignment, white space, and even typography.

## White space
White space or negative space is the empty space in a design. It's the are surrounding the elements. You might be surprised to know that white space is noe necessarily white. Actually, it can be the space in any color or texture.

# How Do You Create Good Background and Foreground Contrast in Your Designs?

## Contrast
First, let familiarize ourselves with contrast. Contrast is the difference between two colors, and how easy it is to tell them apart.

For instance, high contrast colors are white and black, complete opposites very easy to tell apart. Whereas light blue and sky blue are low contrast, hard to tell apart.

Text with a contrast ratio of 4.5:1 is considered the AA standard, which is the bare minimum you should follow to be accessible to most users. Text with a contrast ratio of 7:1 is considered the AAA standard, and ensures the best level of accessibility.

There are three aspects to the contrast ratio

### Hue
The first aspect to the contrast ratio is hue, which represents the general color type, like red, blue, or orange. 

### Saturation
The second is saturation, essentially how much of the color is presence. If you have photos, turning down the saturation turns down the vibrancy of the colors.

### Lightness
Lastly, there is lightness, which represents how much white is present in the color.

# How Does Scale Work in Design?

## Scale
The scale of something refers to its size. When scaling in your web design, you're looking at the size relationships between different elements, and how these elements might adapt to different screen sizes.

Using the correct scale for your elements plays an important role in visual hierarchy. Larger elements will draw more attention, which can guide your users through the content in the way that you want. 

For example, the visual separation between a heading and a paragraph draws your reader's attention, but the scale should be appropriate to get an eye-catching text that pulls your reader to that section.

Scale dosen't apply to just text, its also important for images. While the scale of a banner image might make sense for a desktop layout, it might be too large on a mobile layout. By scaling an image down to a more appropriate ratio, you can keep the visual impact while ensuring the information  on the site is visible. 

Scale is also important for interactivity, and the ability to actually use your website. If the navigation bar is not an appropriate scale, mobile phone users will have a hard time tapping on the links. 

# How Does Alignment Work in Design?

## Alignment
Alignment is the process of arranging text and images in a way that creates a visual connection between elements. It helps to create a sense of order and organization on the page, making it easier for users to navigate and understand the content. 

There are several types of alignment, but the main ones are:
- left alignment
- right alignment
- center alignment
- justified alignment
- vertical alignment

Left, right, and center alignments are all subtypes of horizontal alignment, while vertical and justified alignment are used to align elements along a vertical axis.

Left, right, and center alignments are pretty straight foward so I'll only cover justified and vertical.

Justified alignment is when the text is aligned to both the left and right margins.

Vertical alignments align elements along a vertical axis.

# What Is the Importance of Whitespace in Design?

## White Space
White space referes to any type of space around elements like images, text, and buttons. White space is important in design because it helps to create a balance between the elements on the page.

### Enhancing CTAs
Let's consider call-to-action buttons. CTAs are used to encourage users to take a specific action like signing up for a newsletter or making a purchase.

On the freeCodeCamp homepage, the CTA button is visually seperated from other elements. 

### Types of WhiteSpace
There are different types of whitespace:
- Macro white space is the space between larger elements like images, text, and buttons
- Active white space is the space that is intentionally created to help guide the user's eye and draw attention to certain elements on the page
- Passive white space is the space that is left over after all the elements on a page have been placed
- Micro white space is the space between individual characters in a line of text

### Law of Proximity
When designing your web pages, you always want to be mindful of the law proximity. 

This law states that elements that are close together are percieved as being related, while elements that are far apart are percieved as being unrelated.

# What Are Best Practices for Working with Images in Your Designs?

## Responsive Images
Adding images to your website is a great way to engage your users and increase the visual appeal of your site. But there are a few things to consider when working with images in your designs.

The first thing to consider is creating responsive images. Responsive images that scale to fit the size of the screen they are being viewed on. This is important because it ensures that images look good on ALL devices.

## Resolution
Another thing to consider is the resolution of your image. Higher quality images with better resolution have more pixels per inch. 

Pixerls per inch, or PPI, is the number of pixels oin once inch of an image. The higher the PPI, the better the image quality.

You want to make sure that your images are high quality and look good on all devices. This means that you should use high resolution images that are optomized for the web.

## Size
Another thing to consider is the size of your images and how they fit within the spaces in the layout. You want to make sure that your images are the right size and are not too large or too small.

Using large images that are meant to fit in smalle rspaces in the design can slow down your website and make it harder for users to load your site. You want to make sure that your images are the right size and are optomized for web.

## Balance, Hierarchy, Alignment
Balance is the distribution of visual weight in a design. You wnat to make sure there is a good balance between text and images on the site so it creates a harmonious design.

Hierarchy is the order in which elements are viwed on a page. You want to make sure hta timages that align with important content are placed higher than images that are less important.

Alignment is the place of element in relation to each other. You want to make sure that your images are aligned with the text and other elements on your site so that it creates a cohesive design.

AND DONT FORGET ACCESSIBILITY. Make sure your images are accessible to ALL users, including those with visual impairments.

# What Is Progressive Enhancement?

## Progressive Enhancement
Progressive enhancement is a design appraoch that ensures all users, regardless of browse ror device, can access the essential content and functionality of an application.

The progessive enhancement approach lives by these core principles:
- All core content and basic funcitonality should be accessible to all browsers
- All advanced layouts should be provided through external CSS stylesheets
- All advanced functionality should be provided through external JavaScript files
- A user's browser preferences should be respected.
